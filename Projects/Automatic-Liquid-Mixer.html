Automatic Liquid Mixer

Description:

The automated liquid mixer is a completely innovative system created to revolutionize the process of preparing tailor-made liquid blends. It uses Bluetooth/Wi-Fi to communicate with very precise mechanical operations rather than traditional screens or physical buttons. Orders are only sent from a mobile app or website for user convenience.

After receiving the order from the liquid mix made via Bluetooth or Wi-Fi, it then sends this information over a wired connection to the Arduino Uno through a communication module. This signal flow is said to come from the TX pin of the module to the RX pin of the Arduino. And once the order comes in, it processes instructions that begin by checking the base. The base, a 20x10 cm platform, is 3D-printed, and such a base is where the cup is placed. A contact switch connected to pin 2 of the Arduino informs whether the contact switch had been placed correctly. If the base is not put in place correctly, Arduino moves the stepper motor that moves the base backward until it touches the contact switch. It uses Bluetooth/Wi-Fi to communicate with very precise mechanical operations rather than traditional screens or physical buttons. This signal flow is said to come from the TX pin of the module to the RX pin of the Arduino. And once the order comes in, it processes instructions that begin by checking the base. The base, a 20x10 cm platform, is 3D-printed, and such a base is where the cup is placed. A contact switch connected to pin 2 of the Arduino informs whether the contact switch had been placed correctly. If the base is not put in place correctly, Arduino moves the stepper motor that moves the base backward until it touches the contact switch. This ensures that the base is aligned properly and gets ready to move to the pump's dispensing position.

The stepper motor is actuated by a stepper driver to move the base to the pump corresponding to any given order. It has its inputs connected from the Arduino to the stepper driver for step and direction signals using pins 3 and 4, while power to the driver is provided directly from an adapter. When the base reaches its appointed pump, the relay activates from the Arduino, which energizes the pump to pump the liquid into the cup. Each pump is installed vertically on a wooden frame and connected to two pipes: one liquid intake and another for dispensing into the cup. This is done for all necessary liquids in the mix, moving the base according to the programmed position of each pump. Once all liquids are dispensed, the base is returned to the home position. The stepper motor runs it backwards until it again touches the contact switch. Once again, the Arduino sends signal (code 1010) to the mobile app or website, notifying that the mix of liquids is ready for picking up. Wi-Fi and Bluetooth are the ordering modes available in the system. This versatility will further allow a seamless connection of the user's device with the mixer. It is a very unique feature that completely replaces age-old ordering means with modern wireless ordering system from conventional ones embedded in some designs.

The basic design now comes equipped with features for the smooth and highly precise movement. It slides along two steel rods with the use of shifter and timing belts, with the stepper motor providing the motive power for motion control. The rods are held in place by 3D-printed holders with that on the right serving as the starting point. The pumps and electronics related to them which include the Arduino, communication module, stepper driver, and power adapter are arranged just by the side of the stepper motor for a neat wiring and operation.

In fact, the automated liquid mixer provides an extremely efficient mode of modernizing the way liquid mixes are prepared. Merging high-end modern communication technologies with highly precise mechanical systems, this then results in operations that are very seamless, accurate, and friendly to the user while significantly abating some of the otherwise high manual workloads that such preparations would normally require.
